## Why Connect Integrations?

GAIA becomes more useful the more it knows about your work. When you connect an integration, two things happen:

1. **GAIA gets context.** It can read your emails, calendar events, tasks, and messages — so it understands what you're working on without you having to explain.
2. **GAIA can take action.** It can draft replies, create issues, schedule meetings, and update tasks directly in the apps you already use.

No more switching between tabs to copy information from one tool into another. Tell GAIA what you need and it handles the rest.

## Connect an Integration

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Integrations page">
    Navigate to **Settings > Integrations** from the sidebar. You'll see all available integrations organized by category, with their connection status.

    <img
      className="rounded-lg"
      src="/images/guides/integrations/integrations-page.png"
      alt="Integrations page showing available apps"
    />
  </Step>

  <Step title="Find the app you want">
    Browse by category or use the search bar to find a specific app. Each integration card shows the app name, a short description, and whether it's currently connected.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Connect">
    Click the **Connect** button on the integration you want to link. For most integrations, this opens the app's authorization page (OAuth) in your browser.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Authorize access">
    Sign in to the app if prompted, then review the permissions GAIA is requesting. Click **Allow** or **Authorize** to grant access. You'll be redirected back to GAIA automatically.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm the connection">
    Once authorized, the integration card shows a green dot — it's connected. GAIA now has access to that app's tools and will use them when relevant in your conversations.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  You can also connect integrations directly from the chat sidebar. Expand the
  **Integrations** section to see your connected apps and quickly link new ones.
</Tip>

## What Happens After Connecting

Once an integration is connected, its tools become available in chat automatically. You don't need to configure anything else.

For example, after connecting Gmail, you can say things like:

- "Draft a reply to the last email from Sarah"
- "Send a summary of today's meetings to the team"
- "Find all unread emails from this week about the Q3 report"

GAIA picks the right tool based on your request. The more integrations you connect, the more GAIA can do without you leaving the conversation.

## Managing Your Connections

### Disconnect an integration

Open the integration's detail view and click **Disconnect**. GAIA immediately loses access to that app — no data is retained from the connection.

### Reconnect after token expiry

Some integrations use access tokens that expire after a set period. If a connection stops working, go to the Integrations page and click **Connect** again. You'll re-authorize through the same OAuth flow and the connection is restored.

<Warning>
  If an integration shows an error status, try disconnecting and reconnecting.
  This resolves most issues caused by expired or revoked tokens.
</Warning>
