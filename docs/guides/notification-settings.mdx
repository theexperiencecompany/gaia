## How GAIA Notifications Work

GAIA sends notifications when something needs your attention — a drafted email ready for review, a new task it created on your behalf, a reminder you set, or a workflow that finished running. Each notification appears in real time and can include actions you can take without leaving the notification panel.

There are four types of notifications based on what triggered them:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="AI-Generated Actions" icon="wand-magic-sparkles">
    Email drafts, calendar events, and tasks that GAIA created proactively. These typically include action buttons so you can confirm, edit, or dismiss directly.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Reminders" icon="bell">
    Time-based alerts you asked GAIA to set. These fire at the scheduled time and can include follow-up actions like marking a task complete.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Workflow Updates" icon="arrows-spin">
    Status updates when a workflow completes or fails. Useful for automations that run in the background.
  </Card>

  <Card title="System Notifications" icon="gear">
    Account-related updates like new integrations being provisioned or background jobs finishing.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Notification Channels

GAIA delivers notifications through multiple channels simultaneously. Each channel can be toggled independently.

| Channel | Where it appears | Enabled by default |
|---|---|---|
| **In-app** | Notification panel inside the GAIA web and mobile apps | Always on |
| **Telegram** | Direct message from the GAIA Telegram bot | Yes (requires linked account) |
| **Discord** | Direct message from the GAIA Discord bot | Yes (requires linked account) |
| **Push (mobile)** | Native push notification on your phone | Yes (after granting permission) |

<Note>
  In-app notifications are always delivered and cannot be turned off. They serve
  as the baseline channel so you never miss something important.
</Note>

## Configure Channel Preferences

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Notification Settings">
    Navigate to **Settings > Notifications** from the sidebar. You'll see each notification channel listed with a toggle switch.
    <img
      className="rounded-lg"
      src="/images/guides/notifications/notification-settings.png"
      alt="Notification settings showing Telegram and Discord channel toggles"
    />
  </Step>

  <Step title="Toggle channels on or off">
    Use the switch next to each platform to enable or disable notifications for that channel. Changes save automatically.

    For Telegram and Discord, the toggle only appears after you've linked your account. If a channel shows "Connect in Linked Accounts to enable," head to **Settings > Linked Accounts** first.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Link external accounts (if needed)">
    To receive Telegram or Discord notifications, connect those platforms under **Settings > Linked Accounts**. Once linked, return to Notification Settings to toggle them on.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Keep at least one external channel enabled (Telegram or Discord) so you get
  notified even when you're not actively using the GAIA app.
</Tip>

## Push Notifications on Mobile

The GAIA mobile app uses native push notifications through Expo. When you first open the app, it requests permission to send notifications.

### Enable push notifications

1. Open the GAIA mobile app and sign in.
2. Accept the notification permission prompt when it appears.
3. Your device is automatically registered with GAIA's notification service.

Push notifications include sound and badge updates by default. Tapping a notification takes you directly to the relevant screen — a todo, workflow, or conversation.

<Warning>
  If you denied the notification permission prompt, you'll need to enable it
  manually in your device's system settings under the GAIA app.
</Warning>

### Managing devices

You can register up to 10 devices for push notifications. If you switch phones or want to stop receiving push notifications on a specific device, sign out of the GAIA app on that device — it automatically unregisters.

## Managing Notification Noise

A proactive assistant can generate a lot of notifications. Here are practical ways to stay on top of them without getting overwhelmed.

### Use bulk actions

Open the notification panel and select multiple notifications to mark them as read or archive them in one step. This keeps your panel clean without handling each notification individually.

### Act on notifications inline

Many GAIA notifications include action buttons — "Preview & Send" on an email draft, "View Task" on a new todo, "Confirm Event" on a calendar suggestion. Use these to handle items directly from the notification instead of navigating to the full feature page.

### Archive what you've handled

After you act on a notification, archive it. Archived notifications are hidden from the default view but remain accessible if you need to reference them later.

<Tip>
  Get into the habit of clearing notifications at the end of each day. A clean
  notification panel makes it easier to spot new items that need your attention.
</Tip>

## Notification Actions

GAIA notifications are interactive. Depending on the notification source, you may see action buttons that let you respond directly:

| Source | Available Actions |
|---|---|
| **Email draft** | Preview & Send (opens an editor to review and send the draft) |
| **Calendar event** | Confirm Event (creates the event on your calendar) |
| **New task** | View Task, Mark Complete |
| **Reminder** | Context-specific actions based on what the reminder is about |
| **Workflow** | More Details (navigates to the workflow or conversation) |

Actions that call an API (like confirming an event or completing a task) can only be executed once. The button disables after execution to prevent duplicates.

<Note>
  If an action fails, you'll see an error message. You can retry by navigating
  to the relevant feature page (Todos, Calendar, etc.) and performing the action
  manually.
</Note>
