## Overview

GAIA connects to your Gmail account and turns natural language into real email actions. Read your inbox, draft replies, search for specific threads, organize with labels, and set up automated triage — all from the chat interface.

No tab-switching. No inbox fatigue. Just tell GAIA what you need.

## Connect Gmail

Before GAIA can work with your email, you need to link your Gmail account.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Integrations">
    Navigate to **Settings > Integrations** from the sidebar. Find **Gmail** under the Communication category.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Connect and authorize">
    Click **Connect**, sign in to your Google account, and grant GAIA the requested permissions. You will be redirected back automatically once authorized.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Start using email in chat">
    Once connected, GAIA has access to your Gmail tools. No extra setup required — just ask for what you need in the chat.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  For a full walkthrough of the connection process, see the
  [Connecting Integrations](/guides/connecting-integrations) guide.
</Tip>

## Reading Your Inbox

Ask GAIA to fetch and summarize your email. Be specific about what you want — the more detail you give, the more useful the result.

**Example prompts:**

- "Show me my latest 5 emails"
- "Do I have any unread emails from this week?"
- "Find emails from sarah@acme.com about the Q3 proposal"
- "Show me the full thread with David about the partnership deal"
- "What emails have attachments from the last 3 days?"

GAIA uses Gmail search syntax under the hood, so you get the same filtering power you would in Gmail itself — by sender, date range, labels, read status, attachments, and more.

<Note>
  When fetching full email content, GAIA limits results to 30 messages at a
  time to keep responses fast. If you need more, ask GAIA to fetch the next
  page.
</Note>

## Drafting and Sending Email

GAIA defaults to a **draft-first workflow**. When you ask it to write an email, it creates a Gmail draft so you can review the content before anything leaves your inbox.

**Example prompts:**

- "Draft a reply to the last email from Mark"
- "Write an email to team@company.com summarizing today's standup notes"
- "Draft a follow-up to the budget thread — ask when the revised numbers will be ready"
- "Reply to Sarah's email saying I'll have the report by Friday"
- "Forward the latest email from Alex to the #leadership distribution list"

<Warning>
  GAIA will only send an email directly if you explicitly ask it to. Otherwise,
  it saves a draft. You can review and edit drafts in Gmail before sending.
</Warning>

### Working with Drafts

You can also manage your existing drafts through GAIA:

- "Show me my drafts"
- "What drafts do I have saved?"
- "Delete the draft I just created"
- "Update my draft to Sarah — change the meeting time to 3 PM"

## Automated Email Workflows

When you connect Gmail, GAIA automatically provisions two system workflows that run in the background. You can activate or customize them from the **Workflows** page.

### Inbox Triage

Polls your inbox every 15 minutes. For each new email, GAIA:

1. Classifies it as spam, transactional, newsletter, informational, important, or action-required
2. Skips spam, transactional messages, and newsletters entirely
3. Extracts action items and deadlines from important emails
4. Creates todos for anything that needs follow-up
5. Sends you a brief summary of what was processed

### Auto-Draft Replies

Polls your inbox every 30 minutes. GAIA identifies emails that warrant a response — direct questions, explicit requests, meeting invites, introductions — and saves draft replies in Gmail. Newsletters, automated notifications, and threads you have already replied to are skipped.

Drafts are never sent automatically. You always review and approve before anything goes out.

<Tip>
  You can adjust the polling interval or disable these workflows entirely from
  the Workflows page. They are designed to run quietly in the background and
  only surface what matters.
</Tip>

## Tips for Better Results

<Steps>
  <Step title="Be specific about what you want">
    "Show my unread emails from this week about the budget" works better than "Check my email." The more context you provide, the fewer round trips GAIA needs.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Name people and topics">
    "Find the thread with Alex about the API migration" gives GAIA a clear search target. Vague requests like "find that email from last week" may return too many results.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Tell GAIA when to send vs. draft">
    If you want an email sent immediately, say so explicitly: "Send an email to Jordan saying the contract is signed." Otherwise, GAIA creates a draft for your review.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Chain email with other tools">
    GAIA works across integrations in a single request. "Check my calendar for tomorrow, then email the attendees a summary of the agenda" combines calendar and email in one step.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Let workflows handle the routine">
    Instead of checking your inbox manually every hour, activate the Inbox Triage workflow and let GAIA surface what matters. Use the chat for one-off tasks and targeted searches.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  GAIA learns your writing style and sender context over time through its memory
  system. The more you use email through GAIA, the better its drafts match your
  voice.
</Note>
